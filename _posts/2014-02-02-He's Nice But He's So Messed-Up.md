# "He's Nice But He's So Messed-Up..."

I was asking a friend of mine about her opinion on "The Bro Code" -- specifically the one about dating your close friend's ex, or sister, or someone he really likes/liked. Then our conversation shifted to the series of guys she had dated.

She told me about this ex-boyfriend of her close friend who asked her out. They hanged out for a couple of dates before she called it off. Then there's this other guy who became bitter after she told him they can only be friends. And then there's this guy she went out for 5 dates who thought they were already exclusively dating.

I argued that five dates is already a lot. Or maybe it's because of my age that I no longer want to waste each other's time if it's not going to push through. She said that the guy was nice but he suddenly became possessive, which was a turn off.

I inquired more about the guy. I asked her if it would make a difference if he only got possessive when she's hanging out with her guy friends instead of girls. And she said it won't because he's not her guardian or boyfriend anyway. And I agreed.

She continued telling me the embarrassment that the guy did, like getting drunk and crying in front of her. Then the guy picking himself up by not communicating for a long time. Then after several years, he saw her again while driving and he stopped to ask her out again. She said "no." He tried again for several times but she just kept on rejecting him.

What if he's a changed man? What if he has overcome his insecurities? Doesn't that mean that she's passing up on an opportunity of having a good boyfriend? I mean, he must really like her a lot to be able to withstand that much rejection. And he's a nice guy, after all.

But I already know the answer even before I heard it from her. Because I would have said the same thing. I would not have taken a chance.

I still feel bad for the guy, for some reason. Maybe he deserves to be given a chance. But then again, maybe it doesn't have to be with the same girl. Maybe, in the near future, he'll get another chance and the girl who will be lucky enough to land him when he's no longer the messed-up guy my friend dated. And I'm wishing him all the luck.
