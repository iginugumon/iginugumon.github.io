# The Date-able Girl

I have never signed up to online dating sites. And I have never attended dating events (speed, etc.) Although I would be embarrassed to admit it to anyone I know, I like the idea. You get to talk and weigh people for their dating potential, you are matched to those who share the same interests... And if it didn't work out, at least you have met new people. But there's also this idea that it's only for people who can't land a date on their own. That it's only for losers and the chance of meeting up with someone hot is very slim.

The same goes with getting introduced to someone who might happen to be your match. It's like, "Hey, I'm not that desperate to meet anyone. And I already have so many friends."

So I was a little hesitant when my friend brought up the idea of hooking me up with someone she knows from college. Then she started building her up: she's taking up graduate studies at a well-known university, her family's well-off, she's pretty, and she's nice... And I was like, okay, maybe...

I asked for her name so I can do a little research. I found out that she likes travelling, which is nice because I also love travelling. But she's also not "well-off"; her family is rich -- old rich. And the "well-known university" is a university that you can only enter if you're both rich and intelligent, or if you just happen to be really smart to get a scholarship with stipends to make up for the school's over-the-top expenses. And she's not just "pretty," she's crazy-good looking that you'd wonder if she really does not have a boyfriend. And she also made reference to "The Little Prince"!!!

Then you begin asking questions. "How can she be so good?" "What are her flaws?" "How much baggage does this girl has?" "She must be damaged, right?" "Maybe she's a psycho?"

The last one's funny.

And, knowing I am that much of an overanalyzer, I could be wrong.
