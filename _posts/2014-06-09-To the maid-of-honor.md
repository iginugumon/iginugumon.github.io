# To the maid-of-honor

We were introduced the other night by a good friend of mine. I'm sorry, I honestly did not notice that our common friend took an effort to approach your table just so we could be acquainted. I thought you just happen to come by to the bar.

After you left with your best friend, my friends slammed my behavior. I was wrong. I was supposed to talk to you after the introduction as form of courtesy but I didn't.

My personality type find it hard to talk to people. I'm an introvert. I'd rather rot in a corner than engage in conversations with strangers. I'm pretty good at small talks whenever I feel like I had to do it. But in most cases, I avoid small talks like plague. I view it as an unnecessary exertion of effort. I only do it out of duty.

So when I saw that you already have your friend to talk to, I decided that talking awkwardly is no longer needed.

Again, I hope you did not take it personally. I just thought that you're already good to go because you already have somebody to talk to.

I didn't really mean to be rude. The situation was forced and I didn't know what to do or say. I do not get introduced to a lot of people.

And when I found out what's the deal, I felt bad. I felt bad that I have not been that much of a gentleman. I also felt bad that I have let my friend down.

So when our common friend requested that I ask you to join us for a shot, I went without batting an eyelash.

It's not much. This apology is not even enough. But if ever we do find ourselves in another situation wherein we could talk, I promise you a good conversation.
