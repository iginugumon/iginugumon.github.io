# El Nido, Palawan - Day One

Today is the start of my week-long birthday celebration!

I left Manila at around 8AM and waited for my executive coach at Puerto Princesa. But due to some technical problems with the van, I transferred to a regular van.

The trip from Puerto Princesa to El Nido is not very eventful. The view is not that great. Most of the road is just horrible. Although, in all fairness to Palawan’s local government, you can see that they are making proper improvements to promote tourism. We stopped over at two points: one is to eat lunch, while the other is to wait for passengers.

I alighted at El Nido’s marketplace to buy myself flipflops as I did not bring any. Then I hired a tricycle to take me to town. I asked to be dropped at La Banane Hostel. It turned out that they were closed, I’m not sure if only because it was off-peak season. So, I walked for a bit to The Alternative Inn and, to my dismay, they were closed as well. So I asked around for directions to Our Melting Pot hostel.

I was hoping for a beach front accommodation because I imagined myself waking up to sunrise by the beach. And I thought I had to walk or ride a vehicle from the town proper to the beach. It turned out that the town is so small that I was still happy that I was not able to get a place fronting the beach.

OMP also happens to be such a great place with very friendly staff. I checked into their 10-bed dorm. And another thing that I am very glad about is that the room has an air-conditioning. Plus, it has its own comfort and shower room. All showers have hot water during hours with electricity. You can also have your own locker for your valuables. And you can also borrow a towel for a fee. And, oh, there’s breakfast!

It was already afternoon when I arrived at El Nido and I felt famished. So, I headed towards the beach to find something to eat right after taking a shower. I went to Mezzanine for my dinner.
The place has a great view of the beach. It also has a fine dining beach restaurant feel. But the pizza I ordered was somewhat not as delicious as its price. Even the coke is so small that I really laughed when it arrived. I felt ripped off. But again, the aesthetics of the place is great. And it also has some good reviews from Trip Advisor, so I’m thinking I might not have ordered the right food. I was also bothered by the music but, then again, why am I doing write ups at a bar?
