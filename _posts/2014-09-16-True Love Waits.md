# True Love Waits

What is "true love?" I could write a long discourse on that topic and some might still not agree with me. It's definition is subjective and defining it can only spawn more arguments. So I'll just be discussing the next word: "waits."

What is "waiting?" What does it mean to wait?

Let's say you've been craving for a Krispy Kreme Honey Glazed doughnut for sometime now. Then you see one of their stores at the mall. But there's also this ridiculously long queue of buyers lined up. Would you still buy your doughnut?

Maybe you won't, especially if you hate long lines. But if you really like Krispy Kreme that much, you will wait -- despite the queue.

While waiting in line, a lot of scenarios could happen: I call them "trials in waiting."

You just had a bad day. On normal occasion, you won't be able to stand waiting. Still, you justified your craving. It's the more reason for you to buy what you want. Because it can be the only good thing that you can have for this day.

You've been waiting for an hour already then you realize the line is still too long and you really hate long lines? Nah, you're already salivating. You got to have that doughnut.

Then there's your phone vibrating. You picked up and someone reminded you that you also have an appointment. Will you leave? It always boils down to priorities. You choose between the appointment and the doughnut, whichever is more important.

What if there's this annoying person nearby? How about 2 or 3 annoying persons? Ignore, ignore, ignore. They won't matter afterwards. Your focused on your goal! Oh, the taste of sugar will drown all these people later on. Either that or you'll drown them yourself.

You've survived the past 2 hours. You're still waiting and you're tired of it. Your feet are aching already. But now you're asking yourself "to what ends?"

You're still going to wait as long as you can. You need that doughnut. You want that doughnut. You've already waited long enough that it means the world to you now. But, the world, really???

True love waits. But can waiting for that craving to be satisfied be likened to true love? Most that has driven you so far are just emotions. You're maybe hungry. You might be lacking sugar. Maybe it's just a spur of the moment that you wanted it. Or maybe because you've waited too long to let go.

What if they suddenly ran out of stock of ingredients? A machine malfunctioned? How about the closing time is drawing near and you're still far from giving your orders? You will now be facing the hardest trial of waiting. Something that you will most likely fail. It is the feeling of hopelessness.

The longer it takes to get things going, the more hopeless you get, and the harder it is to keep waiting.

Perhaps you will be lucky and there will be available ingredients later on. Or perhaps the repairman will be successful in fixing the machine. Or perhaps the line will shorten and you will be able to buy that last doughnut. Or perhaps not. Is the doughnut still worth it if complimented with a hot cup of black coffee?

Waiting takes its toll. Hopelessness is never kind. You will break, eventually.

What is true love then if it is not the same as getting your delicious doughnut? What is it if it is still not worth all the hardships you've gone through while waiting?

Waiting is not always true love. Waiting too long for that doughnut is still something you just do out of selfishness.

So, let us change your purpose on getting the doughnuts. Let's say you have a goddaughter whom you love. And every year, you buy her a dozen Krispy Kreme Honey Glazed doughnut on her birthday.

You can be lucky that you came to the store and there's no queue. Or you might go through everything I've enumerated above. You will be annoyed at people. You will get angry. You will get tired. You will get impatient. Yes, you will also feel the hopelessness.

When you're given a bit of hope, you will hold on to it. Even when there's only one doughnut remaining and there are still people ahead of you -- you feel like there's no longer hope, you will still push through.

Until you get to the end of the line.

Until the store closes.

Even when you're not able to get any doughnut despite the wait.

Because one thing that hasn't changed and will never change: seeing the smile on her face is golden. It will be more than worth the wait.

That's true love. You'll wait.
