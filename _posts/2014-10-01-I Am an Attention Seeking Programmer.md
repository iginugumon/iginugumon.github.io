# I Am an Attention Seeking Programmer

I hate the work culture of my company. Either you're overworked, or you do not have any work at all.

I've heard a few of horror stories about being benched from friends in a well-known company for hoarding the entire graduate workforce. Just the thought of it made me cringe. You were a young, fresh graduate looking for something you can work on as a career but all you're given is a whole six month long of just doing nothing. No trainings. Definitely no playing or watching videos. Nothing. Not even a notice from the management on when your first project will be starting.

Now I'm working for years now as a professional. I just happen to be in a company that doesn't know how to efficiently manage its resources (a.k.a. workforce). Some are being burned-out, working from sunrise to sunrise. While some are spending their days juggling mobile games. And I'm currently part of the latter. It sucks.

So there's this one project that I'm not really a part of. They have this problem and I have already solved it. But I'm also too proud to tell them that I want to help and, at the same time, I also don't want to join their nightly crusades. So I just tried meddling.

Of course, they just ignored me. I kept on butting in, but to no avail. Haha! My ego is hurt. Hey, I'm the rockstar of this company! Listen to me!

I feel like they'd talk about me later on when they're having lunch. I don't care.

They kept on talking about their different approaches and different scenarios they'd already pictured in their small heads. But the problem is they failed to ask themselves: "What is the problem?" It's very obvious. And if you'd only look into it as a real world problem and think of how you'd solve it, sans the talks of codes, the solution will be easy.

Let me give an analogy to the issue:

The problem given is not allowing the sum of the amounts to become 100. And we currently have these numbers:

10 19 43 23

The scenarios that they kept on coming up with are the numbers to be added after 23. What if the user adds 5 after 23? How about 6 after 23? How about when the user adds a 3 after 23 and then a 7 after 3? 1 after 23?

But they failed to consider the insertion of a number in between or before the first number, which is 10 in this case.

It's not actually about scenarios or who came up with a lot of it. In a problem you don't really deal with scenarios, you deal with the problem. This is why a lot of programmers are mediocre. They think more with syntax than with basic logic. But this post is becoming me ranting on how my face would always be red if I'll be doing actual facepalms at the office.

Going back to my douche issues. I was doing nothing and I was itching to workout my mind. I have nothing else but my teammates' project. I meddled -- or I tried to. Just like giving out an unsolicited advice to another person.

Truth is, it is embarassing. It was unclassy. I'd hate myself if I were in their place. I hate myself even now that I'm in my place.

I have these attention-seeking tendencies whenever I'm idling on something I'm really good at. Not because I feel like I'm better than them (although I do know that I am better than them), but rather because I needed an outlet. At their IQ's expense and at my face's expense.
