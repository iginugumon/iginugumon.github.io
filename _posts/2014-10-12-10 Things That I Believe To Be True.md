# 10 Things That I Believe To Be True

First off, I know that I'm still in love with you. And I no longer want to elaborate or sour-grape or whatever. It is what it is.

Second is I'm not a planner user. I have bought planners about thrice now and it never worked for me. The space for every date is too small for words. And I also tend to waste the spaces alloted for dates I don't have posts on.

Third, I'm good at programming. No, I'm actually great at it. It is one of the things I know I can kick a lot of people's ass at. This is why my employers love me.

Fourth is I dislike going to school or work during summer time. Summer means heat. I can only tolerate heat when I'm in the country side or beach. I prefer a cool, but dry, weather.

Fifth is I'm tired of being in a corporate world. The 8 to 9 hours of desk job exhausts me. I hate it when I have to consider my co-workers ego even though I know how wrong they are or how awful their ideas are. And I hate the rush hour.

Sixth is I do not want to pursue a romantic relationship with my ultimate crush because I'm afraid. It's a nice thought that there's a chance that we're going to end up together but I'm more afraid of the chance that we're not. I can handle the rejection. What I'm more afraid of, though, is losing an ultimate crush -- either because someone might break the other person's heart or I will be disillusioned of the idea that she's the greatest girl.

The seventh thing that I know to be true is I'm afraid of getting older. I'm afraid that a day will come when I no longer remember a lot of things. Or that I can't even wipe my own ass after taking a crap. Or not being to go to places because I can't even stand. Or not being able to read books because I have very weak eyes.

Eighth would be, if I were to die in the next few days, I would spend the rest of my life in my grandmother's house in Batangas. I'd spend it all by eating, sitting, reading, walking, talking with my relatives, and sleeping.

Ninth is my most favourite book is probably Charles Dickens's "Great Expectations." It is because I see humanity in it. The character development of Pip is so realistic.

The tenth thing that I know to be true is, if the best medicine is laughter, the best healer would be time.
