# My Brother, the Keeper

I was talking to my brother the other night. He was inquiring regarding the cost of accommodation of one country because he's planning to look for a job there. I gave him my inputs and we exchanged words with regards to what should be done.

During the talk, I felt that pang of sadness that he might be going away soon. But, at the same time, I'm also hoping that he can make it. I wish him all the success.

Our family does not actually have the same setup as most families from our country. We grew up separately from our mom. One of my brother had to spend most of his childhood in the province. My sister grew up in the other room and when she became college, she was sent to one of the university's campuses in the province.

And the brother that I was talking about is not exactly my best friend. But we did grew up together. We fought brutally when we were kids, to the point that my mom gave us both knives so we can kill each other. We hated each other. But we also learned to love each other despite our differences. He can be really annoying and I know he also can't stand my presence sometimes. We learned to deal with our quirks as we grew up. We know when to shut-up to not worsen the situation or when not to cross the other person. There are times when we share each others thoughts and dreams. I believe he's the one I have the closest ties with when it comes to the whole family.

In school, I was regarded as the smarter one. I have achieved greatness at such a young age and I did not have a clue back then about how he dealt with that. He strived though. He became the best in his batch due to his diligence. But I still got most credit, even though I was already messing around at that time.

From high school to college, I was the family black sheep. He was the model son. I have received more chances that I did not deserve. And I was very lucky that he understood.

I had the privilege of studying in one of the most expensive schools in the country. He settled with the less expensive, yet competetive university. He kept silent the whole time. I felt the embarassment whenever I see him on his uniform. Yet, I still kept on flunking my subjects.

I stopped going to school when I felt I needed a break. He finished his degree before I did. I think he also got his license before I decided to complete my education. He has always been the better sibling.

If there's one thing he is very famous for in our circle, it is his thriftiness. He has always been the wealthiest. He can save his school allowances for years. He has always been the run-to-person during emergencies. Even during non-emergency situations. And during times when I had to pay for my tuition.

On career and work, I have always been the easy-go-lucky one, while he was the one who works harder. I complain whenever I don't get to leave the office on the dot. He leaves home early and arrives late. He has ventured in different industries. I work 2 hours tops per day. He works even when he's at home. He also ventured in business. It's just that our country does not have enough opportunities. I only happen to be really lucky to be great at something lucrative at this time and age.

Now he's leaving and it breaks my heart. But I should not hold him back. For once, I'm going to be the older brother.
