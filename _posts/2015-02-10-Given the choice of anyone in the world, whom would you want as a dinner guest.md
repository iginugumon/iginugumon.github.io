# Given the choice of anyone in the world, whom would you want as a dinner guest?

I'd like to have the current Pope Francis for dinner. I'm not a Catholic. Heck, I'm not even a theist. But I've been to the side of the religious on certain points of my life and I know how much his reforms are opposed to the teachings on the Bible.

I'm actually for those things that he said. Equality, respecting other religions, family planning, etc. Those are great leaps that was probably met by a lot of protests. But those are also progressive ones that the modern intellectuals might embrace.

But again, they go against what is being taught by their holy book. And I am wondering what pushed him to say those things.

The religion topic falls somewhat near the taboo line for me. It has its way of breaking up friends, cutting ties. But since he seems a rational person, it is one of those moments when I can probably talk and ask and argue about sensitive topics. When we can have a full discussion, presenting our facts, with much respect to the other person's opinion and belief.

It is also probably one of those rare moments when I can ask an old and wise person about life. How one handles the pain of solitude. How to cope with growing older and the thoughts of getting feeble as time goes by. How to put other people before onesself. How to love.
