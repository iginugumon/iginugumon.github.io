# Musings About A Brother Leaving And A Self That Has Already Left

**20150128**

Last night, my mom sent me a text message asking how I am and when will I be able to visit. I replied stating that I'll try to this up coming weekend. I was not able to read her response minutes later as I was alread dozing off. It said that my brother is set to leave on Monday -- I think it's for his long awaited Singapore employment.

I was awakened about 2AM this morning and then I was able to read her message. It got me thinking.

November last year, I talked to my parents that I'm moving out of their house. It was a decision made mainly because I was having a hard time commuting to and from the office. At times it's easy breezy. But there are times when the traffic is your usual Metro Manila traffic that shaves a lot of your life in terms of commuting hours. Another reason is the thought of developing a more mature and independent personality. So, I moved out.

Moving out helped a lot. I've learned to be more responsible. And it also made me happier as I now have a lot of free time to read, watch, sleep, shop, hangout...

A few weeks back my mom told me that my father mused that they've been expanding the house but their children are leaving. My mom said that it'll come to that eventually. She seems to be taking it well.

So the text message from last night made me think that they do miss me after all and I should probably visit this weekend. But the message afterwards made me realize that she's probably a bit depressed about this whole thing.

I think it's so sudden. I left on November and my brother will be leaving the first week of February. They need me. They need to know, now more than ever, that their kids are still within their reach.
Our family setup was a bit weird, which is why I didn't think it's going to be a big deal. We're not really a closely-knit family. And our setup is even worth another post.

I used to think that they've passed on the chance of us getting closer a long time ago. A lot of chances, actually. When they finally decided that it's time, it was already years too late. But I also find myself defending their actions.

* * *

**20150129**

So, past forward to now, which is a day after I typed the above portion of this long post.
Last night, I talked to brother-kun. I asked him about the details of his upcoming departure. Where is he going to stay, what else does he need, what time does he leaves...

I also mentioned our mom. And we decided that maybe it's time for them to learn about the internet. It's one way for them to reach us despite the distance. Our posts about women, profanities, and booze be damned.
