# Three Dancers

Three foreigners: two girls and a guy. They were dancing to the music using the same moves all over again.

My friends and I are travelers spending the night on this suburban town. Tad foreign. But nowhere near as foreign as they are.

We are drinking beers while singing with the live band. We are also dancing on our seats and teasing each other to bring it to the dance floor. We never had that much courage.

Being in a city that increases inhibitions and social stigmas. Where dancing should be in a club or a bar while an EDM is playing. That's the environment we grew up in. Now, we're giving out excuses. I don't dance. A friend says he's not gonna go the floor if no one's joining him. A lady friend is saying the same thing. Others are just sitting, swaying, waving.

And there they are, the three foreigners, dancing gaily to the music. The music changes from fast to slow to fast to medium to slow to medium to fast. Beer on one hand, feet stomping. They made sure to never miss a beat. The prettier one is not even wearing a shoe wear. They were pulling other people from the crowd to join them. Making us feel like strangers to our own country.

Three foreigners, two girls and a guy. They were dancing to the music using the same moves all over again -- moves even I can pull off. With wide smiles on their faces. I see fun. I see freedom.
