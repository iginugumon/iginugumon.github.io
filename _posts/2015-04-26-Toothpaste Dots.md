# Toothpaste Dots

I have been a casual traveler for sometime now. I used to bring that big, bulky toothpaste. Now, I just find a sachet from the sari-sari store in wherever I'll be going, along with sachets of my other toiletries such as shampoo, conditioner, facial wash, etc. It was a great way to travel light. Less weight and less space. Plus, you can buy those small packs at almost anywhere in the Philippines.

But one major problems I've had with sachets is the spills. Once you've opened one, you need to deal with it not getting spread all over the place. I have found myself either consuming everything in one shower, leaving the opened item in the shower for later consumption, or giving it out to other people you're with for consumption.

In the case of toothpaste, I have found one solution: the toothpaste dots. I just need to let the toothpaste dry out in an aluminum (although I have used a plastic container for my first try). The sizing can be done during the spreading or after it dried out already. Then dip the dried products into baking powder to avoid them sticking onto each other during storage. Then you can store them in a small container -- I'm using tic-tac bottles.
