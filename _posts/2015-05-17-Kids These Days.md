# Kids These Days

A friend sent me a text message about hearing kids talking about adult topics while spewing out profanities. So I thought about composing a reply through a blog post.

* * *

Dear S,

Do you know what annoys most about Christians? It's the holier-than-thou attitude. There's a reason why Mahatma Ghandi said once, "_I like your Christ, I do not like your Christians. Your Christians are so unlike your Christ._"

I have this ex-girlfriend who asked me before we became a couple if I am an atheist. I was not then. But I was borderline unbeliever because I no longer really care. So, I lied.

A couple of years ago, I tried going back to church. I met great guys in a group. They're actually pretty nice and all. I see myself from years ago in them. But everytime they mention something about people outside the organization, I find myself smirking. It's because I find it unclassy that they're talking about other people. I find the statements dropped too judgy. They didn't even know those people.

Going back to your case, your statements are too judgemental. You may say that you didn't mean it that way. Yes, you do have good intentions. But the train of thought that leads you to those statements is a mindset of a typical Christian. That either you're with us, or you're going down with the enemy. You may deny it. But I tell you, I have been there. I grew up in church's teachings. I could even defend your faith in debates, even now.

But I digress.

Let me just tell you something you'll probably hear from the granny-level people: Give them a break. They're kids. They have all the time in the world to play know-it-all or be wrong till they get to their own figuring-things-out phase.
