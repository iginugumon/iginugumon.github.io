# MBFW

I remember when a friend told me that her all time favorite film is "My Best Friend's Wedding." I didn't get it. I've heard that in the movie the bestfriends didn't end up together.

"It's sacrifice, you see?" She said like it's the most romantic thing in the world. And I still didn't get it.

I know of sacrifice. I've had my share of it a lot of times before for those I have loved. It's an unpleasant feeling, but you wouldn't have it any other way when you're in the situation.

The film itself is great. It's cheesy -- but it's still a great film. The storyline is solid, comedic scenes are well pulled out at the right moments, the ensemble cast is superb. I love the movie. Believe me, I have watched it a lot of times. And I understand completely that the beauty of the said movie must be because Julianne had to give Michael up as it goes along with the cliché of the phrase, "I only want your happiness."

But isn't it sad to have it as a favorite? Can't they just end up together? Or maybe choose a different film, Something that ends where everybody's happy?
