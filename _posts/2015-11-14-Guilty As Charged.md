# Guilty As Charged

Today, my manager told me that someone said that I'm checking in codes that were not tested. I'm guilty as charged, which is why I didn't bother arguing. But it leaves a bad taste in my mouth, nonetheless. It felt like I wanted to strangle someone the whole morning.

I've been in this industry long enough to know that software developers cut corners at times. This usually happens to companies that are not after getting certified in standardization of processes. There are times when projects require fast turnovers that we skip some steps, like QA testing, in order to be able to deliver faster. And it's wrong. Given the choice, I would prefer following a standard procedure.

But there are tasks that requires abrupt delivery, such as what we were doing these past couple of weeks. And if I were to pass it on to our testers after the development, it could take days before release. And I would probably be scolded more for doing it 'the right way' because of the urgency of the matter.

I was also thinking about the person who reported me. That person is probably a developer too because all the issues I've worked with our Business Analyst never skipped anything, thanks to our issue tracking web application. That developer is also someone I've been working with on the same tasks because he knew that the codes I have checked in are for those items and that they were not tested in QA.

Now, the tasks that I didn't pass on to QA testing are the urgent ones. Those are the ones that requires delivery on the same day. And all of us involved in those never passed them to QA testing. There are also other issues that everyone in this team has cut corners on. So, who are they to cast the first stone?

Not to mention how incompetent they are and how mediocre their works are. But I digress.

I figured the only reason why I'm so pissed about it is because it felt like I'm the only one under fire. The only thing that's stopping me from laying all my cards is fact that I don't want to waste my composure over a bunch of morons. But if it comes down to that, everything I've done is fully documented with complete testing on 3 different environments. I could shove it down their throat if they want to.
