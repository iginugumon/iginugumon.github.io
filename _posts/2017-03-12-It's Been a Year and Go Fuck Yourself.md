# It's Been a Year and Go Fuck Yourself

I looked around the bar and I saw you. I have learned that we are working at the same area and this meeting is inevitable. You were already looking at me, smiling.

I remember that smile. You have always had the watery eyes when you smile. Your head also tend to lean a little bit on the right side. As a whole it is the kind of thing that makes you feel pleasant. At the same time it has the effect of pleading.

I remember the tight embrace when you were pleading me not to leave. I was heartbroken because you wanted to cut it off and we were just starting. I was disappointed that everything's going in favor of the other guy. I guess I already know at the back of my mind but I can't get into terms with the idea.

I was hurting and my heart breaks even more when your strength finally left you and you were just there, weak, no longer wanting to move. I haven't seen you so broken. But then again you broke me first.

I started to break when you can't even tell him to fuck off. I tried to be civil and kept my class until I can no longer do. When all the calls, messages, beers, stinky stares and indifference are just too much for me. And you were cruel and selfish to even notice it.

You were cruel when you kept on seeing him after that. You were selfish by trying to keep us both when you should have already let me go.

You finally let me go. But not until I let you go first.

It was you who saw me first. You were smiling at me when I saw you. I looked around again at the bar and started walking to the door.

No. Nothing's forgotten. You are not forgiven.
