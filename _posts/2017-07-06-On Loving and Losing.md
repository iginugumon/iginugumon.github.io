# On Loving and Losing

Dear Ellie,

I have always been looking forward to your letters and you can imagine my excitement when I saw another one arrived. But the news is also saddening.

Losing people we love is one of the most common things to happen in life. People come and people go. It is something we should have gotten used by now, and we probably are. But losing people we care most about will always be painful. It hurts when we were still kids. And it hurts still, now that we are older and wiser. The pain is there no matter how better we became at handling the situation.

Last year I have received your letter about the gift of losing someone. It was right after I lost someone I love. I was already in the healing process at that time. And now, even though I have completely recuperated, I can still remember how excruciating when the issue was still at its peak. How I said yes to travels I was not ready financially just to get it out of my head. I am glad I did because those trips were wonderful. I hope you get to enjoy yours too.

Just like you've said, not everyone is given the gift of losing someone. Not everyone gets to experience life as we are experiencing it. And feeling all these emotions is maybe not a part of something bigger, but at least it is life.

I don't know how you are holding up. But I do hope that you're doing fine.

Thank you for sharing these things through your letters. I'd be glad to hear from you again.

Much love,
