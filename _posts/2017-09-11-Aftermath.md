# Aftermath

I believe I have never written a formal article about you.

Everything happened so fast. The blow was hard that it took a while to recuperate. Everything that I have felt back then was poured into different mediums: Facebook comments, Facebook messenger chats, Twitter posts. Posts came in irregular, small bursts. But no full article was finished. The moment that I can finally express myself in whole was when I have already recovered. That time, it felt like I still needed a disconnect. Despite my fast recovery, I was still not ready.

Now I must write this obligatory piece. Not as closing of a chapter, but more of recollection of events and coming into terms of what I have felt back then and how I still feel about it.

I was doing okay when you came. I have a good-paying job, work schedule is tolerable, a bunch of great friends, etc. The discontent is mostly on the career category. And there was no feeling of a need of a lover.

Our first interaction was brief and not that notable. We've thought of the other person as boring and unimpressive. On my part I thought you were naive about choosing someone who's already dying as your president. Your work also shows how much of unachiever you are despite your alma mater. To be fair, you have asked our common friends about me and despite all the build ups from them you found me bland. I think I sent a "good night" with no intention of talking to you again.

A couple of months passed and suddenly we're talking again. The tone have changed, though. That time around I found you interesting because of your music and movie preferences. Which, in turn, prompted me to bring out my repressed social skills.

The first time we met you said you got conscious with your get-up. It's flattering as majority of the population don't really see me as attractive. Then one meeting led to another and another. The next thing I know we are already drawn to each other and I have asked you, trying to be a gentleman, if you wanted to make things formal.

The thing is, I usually don't go all out on girls. Even though I'm already going out with someone I always say it's just something casual. I don't want to commit into a relationship if I am not sure if I want it. To ask you that question must have meant that my overthinking self must have thought a lot about it already.

But you said you were not ready. I should have seen the red flag.

We went on as if we're already getting there. And I didn't really care that we don't have commitments. It was fun and wonderful, regardless of uncertainties.

One day you said you wanted to talk. I complied. And I panicked when you bursted into tears while we were in the restaurant. Then you confessed about your sins. That moment I was too shocked and I didn't want to deal with it.

I could have walked away unscathed. But I didn't. You were too good to pass on. I rarely connect with anyone on that level. And I was ready to give up on good friends just so I can have you. It was a necessary sacrifice and an excruciatingly difficult decision. I chose you.

After a couple of weeks of blissfulness your past came to haunt you. And it haunted me too.

I should have recognized the signs early on that you were already pushing me away. But how can I when every time I let you go you suddenly cling on. I was confused. It should have been easy if only you knew what you really wanted. The tug-o-war of emotions could have been avoided if only you have not been as selfish.

And it took an underhanded move to finally uncover the truth. Then we parted ways.

The whole process was painful. I was devastated. But I am good at moving on. I have a fast recovery. It was not easy. Nothing is ever easy for such scenarios. It took a huge amount of effort from my friends and myself. And it was expensive as well: dates, eating out, travels, etc. Eventually, I started singing again.

I also started hating you. For the lies, the betrayals. I was finally able to see that my friends were right. I was in denial because of our superficial connection. Our relationship was comfortable. And comfort can be easily confused with real.

3 months passed before we saw each other again. You have avoided my glares the whole time. And rightly so. You should have been ashamed of what you did. I wondered where you got the gall to even show yourself. But that very moment I knew I have won.

You were right about one thing: you are not a good person I thought you were and I should be hating you. But the moment of realization came in too late that I can no longer afford to hate you. Because, even when trying to take the things that you did into consideration, those no longer matter. You no longer matter.

From this moment onwards I know I will be retelling the story sparing my listeners the unnecessary details:

"Around the 2nd quarter of 2016 I met a girl. We dated for a while. Then she told me she had an affair with my friend who still had a girlfriend, who also happens to be a friend of mine, when it happened. It stopped momentarily during the time we were going out but it resumed later on. We stopped seeing each other when I learned about it. That was a very difficult time for me. But the world moved on since then. They became a couple. While I was able to rid myself of them. And I also get to keep the great friends I was willing to give up on just so I can have a future with her."
