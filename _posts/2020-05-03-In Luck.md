# In Luck

I have always thought I'd end up with someone I can truly connect with. Someone I can talk with for hours about different topics that would pick each other's brain. We might not agree on some points, but a good discussion will be made. And through the years, I have connected with several. Who knew?

Now, I am married with a woman who likes to talk a lot -- about things I don't really care about. She's kind of a bigot on some issues that it irritates me. I also hate it when she doesn't even know when to stop talking and not give a shit about my alone time. I could say it's not ideal. But to be honest, it's not how I would have wanted.

Before all of this, I have already given up on the idea of getting married. It's too much of a mess to be in. If ever I happen to end up with someone I truly like, I might not like that person in the long run. People change, feelings change much sooner. Then I got hitched.

Maybe I was just jaded. Maybe I just gave up. I don't have the answers. 

One day she asked me when are we going to get married. And suddenly it hit me, why not? Maybe it isn't so bad. I realized that no matter how much I love flirting, I was at the point when I could finally let it go. 

To be fair, my wife isn't so bad. She has a lot of great qualities you wouldn't be able to find in other women. And no matter how anyone in their right mind will look at it, I got lucky.
